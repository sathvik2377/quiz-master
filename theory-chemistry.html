Chemistry Theory

Explore the molecular world and chemical reactions

Atomic Structure

Quantum Numbers

  • Principal (n): Energy level (1, 2, 3, ...)
  • Azimuthal (l): Subshell (0 to n-1)
  • Magnetic (m): Orbital (-l to +l)
  • Spin (s): Electron spin (±½)

Electronic Configuration

  • Aufbau Principle: Fill lowest energy orbitals first
  • Pauli Exclusion: Max 2 electrons per orbital
  • Hund's Rule: Fill singly before pairing
  • Order: 1s² 2s² 2p⁶ 3s² 3p⁶ 4s² 3d¹⁰...

Chemical Bonding

Types of Bonds

  • Ionic: Transfer of electrons (metal + non-metal)
  • Covalent: Sharing of electrons (non-metal + non-metal)
  • Metallic: Sea of electrons (metal + metal)
  • Hydrogen: H bonded to N, O, or F

VSEPR Theory

  • Linear: 2 electron pairs (180°)
  • Trigonal Planar: 3 electron pairs (120°)
  • Tetrahedral: 4 electron pairs (109.5°)
  • Trigonal Bipyramidal: 5 electron pairs
  • Octahedral: 6 electron pairs (90°)

Thermodynamics

Laws of Thermodynamics

  • First Law: ΔU = q + w
  • Enthalpy: H = U + PV
  • Entropy: ΔS = q_rev/T
  • Gibbs Free Energy: G = H - TS

Spontaneity

  • ΔG < 0: Spontaneous
  • ΔG = 0: Equilibrium
  • ΔG > 0: Non-spontaneous
  • ΔG° = -RT ln K

Chemical Equilibrium

Equilibrium Constants

  • Kc: Concentration equilibrium constant
  • Kp: Pressure equilibrium constant
  • Relationship: Kp = Kc(RT)^Δn
  • Le Chatelier's Principle: System responds to stress

Acid-Base Equilibrium

  • pH: -log[H⁺]
  • pOH: -log[OH⁻]
  • pH + pOH = 14 (at 25°C)
  • Ka × Kb = Kw

Electrochemistry

Galvanic Cells

  • Anode: Oxidation occurs (negative)
  • Cathode: Reduction occurs (positive)
  • EMF: E°cell = E°cathode - E°anode
  • Nernst Equation: E = E° - (RT/nF)lnQ

Electrolysis

  • Faraday's Laws: m ∝ Q, m ∝ M/n
  • Charge: Q = nF (n = moles of electrons)
  • Current: I = Q/t

Organic Chemistry Basics

Functional Groups

  • Alkanes: C-C single bonds
  • Alkenes: C=C double bonds
  • Alkynes: C≡C triple bonds
  • Alcohols: -OH group
  • Aldehydes: -CHO group
  • Ketones: C=O group
  • Carboxylic Acids: -COOH group

Reaction Mechanisms

  • SN1: Two-step substitution
  • SN2: One-step substitution
  • E1: Two-step elimination
  • E2: One-step elimination
  • Addition: Alkene reactions
  • Electrophilic Aromatic Substitution
