Grammar Fundamentals

Parts of Speech

  • Noun: Person, place, thing, or idea
  • Pronoun: Replaces a noun (he, she, it, they)
  • Verb: Action or state of being
  • Adjective: Describes a noun
  • Adverb: Describes a verb, adjective, or adverb
  • Preposition: Shows relationship (in, on, at, by)
  • Conjunction: Connects words/phrases (and, but, or)
  • Interjection: Expresses emotion (oh, wow, alas)

Tenses

  • Present: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous
  • Past: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous
  • Future: Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous

Sentence Structure

Types of Sentences

  • Simple: One independent clause
  • Compound: Two or more independent clauses
  • Complex: One independent + one dependent clause
  • Compound-Complex: Multiple independent + dependent clauses

Clauses

  • Independent: Can stand alone as a sentence
  • Dependent: Cannot stand alone
  • Noun Clause: Functions as a noun
  • Adjective Clause: Modifies a noun
  • Adverb Clause: Modifies a verb, adjective, or adverb

Common Errors

  • Subject-Verb Agreement: Singular subject + singular verb
  • Pronoun Agreement: Pronoun matches antecedent
  • Dangling Modifiers: Modifier should clearly modify intended word
  • Run-on Sentences: Properly connect independent clauses

Vocabulary Building

Word Formation

  • Prefixes: un-, re-, pre-, dis-, mis-
  • Suffixes: -tion, -ness, -ment, -ly, -able
  • Root Words: Latin and Greek origins
  • Compound Words: Two words combined

Context Clues

  • Definition: Word meaning given directly
  • Synonym: Similar word used nearby
  • Antonym: Opposite word used for contrast
  • Example: Specific instances provided
  • Inference: Meaning implied from context

Synonyms and Antonyms

  • Synonyms: Words with similar meanings
  • Antonyms: Words with opposite meanings
  • Degrees of Meaning: Subtle differences in similar words
  • Connotation: Emotional or cultural associations

Reading Comprehension

Reading Strategies

  • Skimming: Quick overview for main ideas
  • Scanning: Looking for specific information
  • Active Reading: Engaging with the text
  • Note-taking: Recording key points
  • Questioning: Asking about content while reading

Types of Questions

  • Main Idea: Central theme or purpose
  • Supporting Details: Facts that support main idea
  • Inference: Conclusions drawn from text
  • Author's Purpose: Why the text was written
  • Tone and Mood: Author's attitude and atmosphere

Text Types

  • Narrative: Tells a story
  • Descriptive: Describes something in detail
  • Expository: Explains or informs
  • Persuasive: Tries to convince reader
  • Compare/Contrast: Shows similarities and differences

Writing Skills

Essay Structure

  • Introduction: Hook, background, thesis statement
  • Body Paragraphs: Topic sentence, evidence, analysis
  • Conclusion: Restate thesis, summarize, final thought
  • Transitions: Connect ideas smoothly

Writing Process

  • Prewriting: Brainstorming and planning
  • Drafting: Writing the first version
  • Revising: Improving content and organization
  • Editing: Correcting grammar and mechanics
  • Publishing: Sharing the final version

Style and Tone

  • Formal: Academic or professional writing
  • Informal: Casual or conversational writing
  • Persuasive: Convincing the reader
  • Descriptive: Creating vivid images
  • Narrative: Telling a story

Literary Devices

Figures of Speech

  • Metaphor: Direct comparison without "like" or "as"
  • Simile: Comparison using "like" or "as"
  • Personification: Giving human qualities to non-human things
  • Hyperbole: Deliberate exaggeration
  • Alliteration: Repetition of initial consonant sounds
  • Onomatopoeia: Words that imitate sounds

Literary Elements

  • Theme: Central message or meaning
  • Plot: Sequence of events
  • Character: People in the story
  • Setting: Time and place
  • Point of View: Perspective of narration
  • Conflict: Problem or struggle

Poetic Devices

  • Rhyme: Similar ending sounds
  • Rhythm: Pattern of stressed/unstressed syllables
  • Meter: Regular pattern of rhythm
  • Imagery: Vivid descriptive language
  • Symbolism: Objects representing ideas
